Unmatched Speed

When I write code, it happens now and then that I forget some syntactic knowledge, and typically I will ask google (type the question in the chrome address bar) instead of looking up in documentation.

When I use my laptop with a connected mouse, I often turn to touchpad to accomplish small tasks. Actually, it’s known that some proficient linux programmers don’t use either, keeping their hands within the keyboard area.

Why would I make these decisions? Consulting documentation and using mouse are both higher-quality solutions: one can offer more well-structured knowledge, and the other can fully harness powerful modern GUI. I figure it’s because I want the speed of my operation to catch up with that of my thinking flow, and sometimes I just want a short answer, unwilling to interrupt my flow to search for it.
